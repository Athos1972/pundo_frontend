# What is a Mukhtar?

![A Mukhtar speaking with residents of a Cypriot village](/images/guides/mukhtar.png)

A **Mukhtar** (Arabic: مختار, meaning "chosen one") is the elected representative of a village or community in Cyprus. They serve as the first point of contact for local administrative matters and are an important link between residents and government authorities.

## What does a Mukhtar do?

<StepList steps={[
  "Representing the community to government authorities",
  "Certifying local documents and declarations",
  "Assisting with residency registration",
  "Mediating neighborhood disputes",
  "Coordinating with local authorities for community events"
]} />

## When do you need a Mukhtar?

As an expat, you'll most likely need the Mukhtar in these situations:

| Situation | What the Mukhtar does |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Yellow Slip (MEU1) | Confirms your residency in the community |
| Proof of residence | Issues certificates for official purposes |
| Special events | Approves gatherings and events in the village |

<InfoBox type="tip">
  The Mukhtar usually has fixed office hours. Call ahead to avoid waiting. Bring your passport, rental contract, and photos.
</InfoBox>

## Finding your Mukhtar

Contact details for your local Mukhtar can be found at the **Community Office** or on the municipal website. In larger cities like Larnaca, each district has its own Mukhtar.

<InfoBox type="warning">
  A Mukhtar is not a lawyer. For legal matters (contracts, inheritance, disputes), consult a licensed attorney or the municipal legal advice service.
</InfoBox>
